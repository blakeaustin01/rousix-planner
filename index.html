What this prototype does

It makes a complicated idea easy to see: pick a goal, enter the price, enter contribution amounts, review the gap, compare infrastructure options, then prepare the next step.

  • Live roadmap changes as numbers change.
  • Tier suggestion updates from goal, amount, timeline, and selected scenario.
  • Infrastructure cost is deducted before the ownership-path math.
  • Copy and print buttons work for marketing conversations.

Plain explanation

Surplus-based planning means the person is not just asking, “Can I borrow money?” They are asking, “What contribution path could support this ownership goal, and what is still missing?”

The app keeps that message simple: goal, price, starting contribution, monthly contribution, timeline, suggested infrastructure, roadmap, and next step.

What changes the suggested tier?

The suggested discussion tier changes when the goal price, starting contribution, monthly contribution, timeline, or scenario changes. The app estimates what tier is reasonable to discuss by looking at how much contribution capacity exists early in the roadmap and how large the ownership goal is.

How Rousix may help

Rousix can help clarify the planning goal, explain the simple onboarding steps, review the suggested infrastructure tier, and identify what information would be needed before any real next step.

Onboarding can be explained like this: enter basic personal credentials, choose the goal, enter the price, add the starting contribution, add the monthly contribution, review the 24–36 month roadmap, then decide whether to start small or move forward with a larger plan.

Network effect language

As more people participate and more infrastructure is purchased, the network may become more useful. The planner can show education scenarios like 5x, 10x, 15x, or 40x, but those are only examples for discussion. They are not promises.

That lets the marketing prototype talk about upside while staying upfront about uncertainty.

Step 4

Get started

This page uses hosted payment links. The website never sees or stores card numbers.

Simple start path

  1. $1 planning deposit: confirms interest and collects payment details through a hosted checkout page.
  2. $5+ starting contribution: minimum starting amount. Higher amounts can be used for serious plans.
  3. $1+ monthly contribution: minimum recurring rhythm. Larger monthly plans should be reviewed directly.
  4. Roadmap review: print the plan, review concerns, and decide whether to start small or proceed with a larger plan.

Before someone pays
